Living Correctly

  • 1st, January 2024
Life with God is a life truly lived.

The feeling of unfulfillment is something many of us have felt. We get that job we have always wanted, but don’t feel the satisfaction we thought it will bring. For some of us, it’s a relationship that we entered, or money, or even material things. We have all these things, yet they are seemingly unsatisfying, unfulfilling. King Solomon understands this perfectly (Ecclesiastes 2). It’s simply because these things are not designed to bring anyone fulfillment. Although, they could be for our enjoyment, but not our fulfillment.

Every human was created to do life with God, regardless of whether you actually do life with Him or not. The evidence of this is seen from the creation of Adam and Eve till now, which is God’s desire to relate with us. When God created Adam and Eve, He could have just left them there and watched them live, but He did not do that. Instead, he related with them, He told Adam to be fruitful and multiply, that He had created all the earth for his enjoyment, that he should reign over every other creation. When God saw that Adam was lonely, He said ‘it is not good for man to be alone’ and He gave Him a helper. So, if God had intended for life to be lived without Him, He would have just created man and watched Him, without interacting with him, defining things for him, helping him.

All this is to say, that life lived with God, is the only correct way of living. This is what brings a man fulfillment. This is because in doing that, you are living out God’s will. Think about this: Artists, Singers, Song writers, Sculptors or any one in the ‘creators’ field can relate to this. The use of whatever they create is determined by them and what gives those creations fulfillment is that they have been used for the purpose which they are created. In that same way, living life with God and according to His will is what gives us fulfillment.

Also think about the fact that King Solomon was the richest King in the world. He had everything. Wealth, women, fame, wisdom, power, you name it, but this is what he had to say about these things: “But as I looked at everything, I had worked so hard to accomplish, it was all so meaningless-like chasing the wind. There was nothing really worthwhile anywhere.” (Ecclesiastes 2:11).

At the end, life is more than just acquiring things for oneself. Whatever the thing may be. What makes life most meaningful, is God, being the author of life. This new year is the best time to sit back and re-evaluate. How have you been doing life? Have you included God in your decisions? Or do you only include Him in some and decide the rest for yourself? What has mattered most to you these past years. Have you lived out, or in the will of God? If no, you have the chance to change that and start doing life with God this new year.

PRAYER POINT
God, I give my life to you. I receive grace to live correctly and include you in everything. Amen.
